Research

I'm interested in machine learning, image processing, geometric learning, and manifold-based methods in shape analysis. Much of my research is about learning from the physical world (shape, motion, etc) from geometry and shape modeling captured by meshes.

My primary dissertation focus involves modeling the human brain and its several structures (hippocampus, amygdala, white matter, etc.) using mesh manifolds and leveraging the power of graph convolutional networks (GCNs) to assist in a variety of medical applications focused on shape analysis such as:

  • the diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease dementia (ADD) by learning from brain shape
  • generating the structural timeline of a subject's brain given the progression of their ADD diagnosis throughout time
